| [High-Resolution Image Synthesis with Latent Diffusion Models (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.10752) **(Stable Diffusion)**    | [GitHub - CompVis/stable-diffusion: A latent text-to-image diffusion model](https://github.com/CompVis/stable-diffusion)     | **Principle:** <br><br>  The training cost of traditional deep learning models is reduced by utilizing a compressed image representation achieved through an autoencoding model. | [GLIDE: Towards Photorealistic Image Generation and Editing with Text-Guided Diffusion Models (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.10741) **(GLIDE)** | [GitHub - openai/glide-text2im: GLIDE: a diffusion-based text-conditional image synthesis model](https://github.com/openai/glide-text2im) | **Principle** <br><br> In simple words, a Text guided Diffusion model. | [SDEdit: Guided Image Synthesis and Editing with Stochastic Differential Equations (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.01073) **(SDEdit)** | [GitHub - ermongroup/SDEdit: PyTorch implementation for SDEdit: Image Synthesis and Editing with Stochastic Differential Equations](https://github.com/ermongroup/SDEdit) | **Principle :** <br><br> In the diffusion process, rather than beginning with random noise, this model starts with a noised version of the strokes. This approach helps it remain true to the original image while introducing a realistic effect through inpainting. <br><br> **Limitations :** <br><br> The generated output does not vary too much with respect to the input unless you chose a strength value of 1. |
| [Photorealistic Text-to-Image Diffusion Models with Deep Language Understanding (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11487) **(Imagen, DeepFloyd IF)** | [GitHub - deep-floyd/IF](https://github.com/deep-floyd/IF) | **Contributions :** <br><br> - Both models (Imagen and DeepFloyd IF) share common elements; they utilize a text-to-image diffusion model that produces an initial low-resolution image of 64x64 pixels. This image is subsequently upscaled to higher resolutions, first to 256x256 and then to 1024x1024, by two additional models. <br><br> - For the text encoding, both models use Google's large pre-trained Text-To-Text Transfer Transformer (T5), which converts all NLP tasks into a text-to-text format, with both input and output as text strings. The text encoder is a key component in the DeepFloyd IF/Imagen, as the T5 model offers a more extensive understanding of language than CLIP. <br><br> - Imagen introduces an Efficient U-Net for super-resolution models, which is touted as being simpler, faster to converge, and more memory-efficient than previous versions. The modifications to the U-Net, compared to earlier diffusion models, include transferring some parameters from high-resolution to low-resolution blocks (which have a greater number of channels and hold more semantic information relative to the initial blocks), increasing the number of residual blocks at lower resolutions, and changing the sequence of convolution operations in relation to up/downsampling. Specifically, in Imagen, downsampling occurs prior to convolution, while upsampling happens afterward. |
| [Unsupervised Cross-lingual Representation Learning at Scale (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1911.02116) **(Kandinsky)** | [Unsupervised Cross-lingual Representation Learning at Scale (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1911.02116) | **Contributions :** <br><br> - Similar to DALL·E-2, Kandinsky utilizes a prior model (Diffusion Mapping) to forecast CLIP image embeddings from CLIP text embeddings. Moreover, comparable to Latent Diffusion, the diffusion model operates not in pixel space as DALL·E-2/Imagen does, but in latent space. <br><br> - An important difference is that the latest versions, 2.2 and 2.1, of Kandinsky use XLM-RoBERTa as the text encoder, thus making the model multilingual. <br><br> - In contrast to DALL·E-2, the output of the prior model doesn’t go directly into a decoder; instead, it’s first directed towards a latent diffusion model. |
| [Adding Conditional Control to Text-to-Image Diffusion Models (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.05543) (ControlNet) | [GitHub - lllyasviel/ControlNet: Let us control diffusion models!](https://github.com/lllyasviel/ControlNet) | **Principle**: ControlNet is an auxiliary network that manages the output of large diffusion models (DM) by processing conditioning images in various formats, such as Canny edges, depth maps, and sketches. This conditioning is integrated into each block of the DM's decoder. In essence, ControlNet is a trainable replica of the DM's encoder, enhanced with additional zero convolution layers. During training, the DM's weights are completely frozen to prevent overfitting when the dataset is not sufficiently large. <br><br> **Advantages**: <br> - Training ControlNet is comparable to fine-tuning a copy of the pre-trained DM on larger datasets, which minimizes the risk of overfitting. <br> - It is robust against small training sets. <br> - It has the ability to interpret the contents of input images. <br><br> **Disadvantages**: <br> - The images generated by ControlNet do not appear very realistic for our needs. (requires further fine-tuning with a specific method) <br> - ControlNet sometimes has difficulties with human faces. |
| [ControlNet-XS: Designing an Efficient and Effective Architecture for Controlling Text-to-Image Diffusion Models](https://vislearn.github.io) (ControlNet XS) | [GitHub - vislearn/ControlNet-XS](https://github.com/vislearn/ControlNet-XS) | **Principle**: ControlNet-XS is a variant of the traditional ControlNet designed to address specific issues: <br> - **Information Delay**: In the original architecture, ControlNet introduces corrections during the decoding phase of the base model, leading to information delays and complicating the controlling network's task, which must predict and correct the encoder block's errors. <br><br> **Contributions**: <br> - ControlNet-XS incorporates bidirectional connections between the controlling network and the encoder block, simplifying the learning process for the controlling network. The paper demonstrates that this alteration enables the new model to achieve better results with fewer parameters, reduced processing time, and lower memory consumption. |
| [InstructPix2Pix: Learning to Follow Image Editing Instructions (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.09800) (InstructPix2Pix) | [GitHub - timothybrooks/instruct-pix2pix](https://github.com/timothybrooks/instruct-pix2pix) | **Principle**: The method involves three stages: <br> 1. Generate a collection of input captions, editing instructions, and revised captions. <br> 2. Use a technique known as Prompt to Prompt to create a dataset of image pairs linked to the input and edited captions. <br> 3. Train a generative model to execute the desired modification according to the provided instruction. |
| [How Generative Adversarial Networks and Their Variants Work: An Overview (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.05914v9) (GANs) | - | **An overview on GANs fundamentals**: <br> - GANs theoretical background (Principle & Objective functions) <br> - Different variants <br> - Practical & Theoretical issues of GANs training <br> - Some techniques to improve the training and mode collapse in GAN <br><br> **Pros**: <br> - GAN can parallelize the sampling of the generated data. <br> - GAN does not need to approximate a likelihood by introducing a lower bound. <br> - GAN is empirically known to produce better and sharper result than other VAE-inspired generative models. <br><br> **Cons**: <br> - Training of GAN is highly unstable and difficult to converge. <br> - For highly complex and multimodal real data distribution, the mode collapse still remains a problem GAN has to solve. |
| [Analyzing and Improving the Image Quality of StyleGAN (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1912.04958) (StyleGAN) | [GitHub - rosinality/stylegan2-pytorch: Implementation of Analyzing and Improving the Image Quality of StyleGAN (StyleGAN 2) in PyTorch](https://github.com/rosinality/stylegan2-pytorch) | **Contributions**: This paper tries to correct the following problems in StyleGAN: <br> - Solve the consistent problem of artifacts in GANs. <br> - Improve the stability of GANs training. <br> - Enhance the generated images quality. <br><br> **Cons**: This model does not accommodate text prompts inputs to edit/augment the generated images. |
| [Image-to-Image Translation with Conditional Adversarial Networks (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1611.07004) (cGAN) | [GitHub - junyanz/pytorch-CycleGAN-and-pix2pix: Image-to-Image Translation in PyTorch](https://github.com/junyanz/pytorch-CycleGAN-and-pix2pix) | **Contributions**: <br> - Conditional GAN for image to image translation <br> - Add L1 error constraints to force the model to generate images close to the input ones. <br><br> **Cons**: This model does not accommodate text prompts inputs to edit/augment the generated images. |
| [Diffusion Models Beat GANs on Image Synthesis](https://github.com/openai/guided-diffusion) | - | **Comparative study of GANs and DMs**: <br> **GANs**: <br> - Faster processing time because of one forward pass instead of multiple. <br> - Fidelity, GANs can produce high quality images. <br> - Difficult & unstable training. <br> - Restricted generation diversity. <br><br> **DMs**: <br> - Higher diversity in generated images. <br> - Much longer forward pass. |

| [Learning from Simulated and Unsupervised Images through Adversarial Training IEEE Conference Publication IEEE Xplore](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/8099724) | - | **Method**: <br><br> - Use GAN to generate realistic eyes images from synthetic images <br> - The generator is asked to generate images close to the input ones using a self-regularization term. <br> - The discriminator tries to distinguish the fake images from the real images using local adversarial loss to prevent artifacts. <br> - With training, the discriminator pushes the generator to produce more plausible images. <br> - They also implemented a history buffer that saves some old generated images to prevent catastrophic forgetting and help the model converge faster. <br><br> **Discussion**: The method is very suitable for our use case, however, the model they used does not support text prompts and thus needs some modifications to be suitable for synset images to real images augmentation. |
| [Adversarial Supervision Makes Layout-to-Image Diffusion Models Thrive (arxiv.org)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.08815) | [GitHub - boschresearch/ALDM: Official implementation of "Adversarial Supervision Makes Layout-to-Image Diffusion Models Thrive" (ICLR 2024)](https://github.com/boschresearch/ALDM) | **Contributions**: <br> - Training a diffusion model jointly with segmenter-discriminator that classifies pixel by pixel to one of the semantic segments in the image or to the additional 'fake' class. <br> - The discriminator evaluates the generated image in multiple steps of denoising and not just the last one (Multistep Unrolling) to ensure that the generator respects the layout (segments) in each step of the generation. |
